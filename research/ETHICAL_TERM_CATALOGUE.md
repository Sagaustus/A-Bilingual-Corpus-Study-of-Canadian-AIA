## About this Catalogue

This catalogue documents 37 ethical terms drawn from three branches of philosophy
as they appear — and are transformed — in Canadian federal Algorithmic Impact Assessments.
Each entry records:

- The term's **philosophical origin** (tradition, key thinkers, canonical definition)
- Its **corpus frequency** in English and French submissions
- The **drift type** — how and why the term's meaning has shifted from its philosophical origin
- The **cross-linguistic divergence** — how English and French submissions conceptualize
  the term differently from each other
- **Representative corpus sentences** in both languages
- A **key finding** synthesizing the most important insight for each term

**Branch:** Normative Ethics  
**Tradition(s):** consequentialist  
**Key philosophers:** Mill, John Stuart; Feinberg, Joel; Parfit, Derek; Shue, Henry

#### Philosophical Definition

direct, indirect, cumulative, or systemic.
