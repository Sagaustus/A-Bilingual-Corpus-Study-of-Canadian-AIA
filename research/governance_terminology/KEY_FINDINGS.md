# EN/FR Governance Terminology Analysis - Key Findings

**Analysis Date:** January 9, 2026  
**Dataset:** 16 bilingual Canadian AIA document pairs  
**Total Governance Terms:** 751 occurrences  
**Languages:** English (EN) / French (FR)  

---

## 🎯 Executive Summary

Analysis of 16 bilingual Canadian Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA) document pairs reveals **systematic semantic drift** in governance terminology between English and French versions. Most notably, the concept of "accountability" appears **15 times in English** but is **completely absent from French translations**, suggesting conceptual erasure during the translation process.

---

## 📊 Critical Findings

### 1. The "Accountability Gap" 🚨

**Most Significant Discovery**

| Language | Term | Occurrences |
|----------|------|-------------|
| English | accountability | **15** |
| French | responsabilité | **0** |

**Context Examples (English):**
- "ensuring accountability in automated decision-making"
- "accountability mechanisms must be in place"
- "lines of accountability should be clearly defined"

**What appears instead in French:**
- Generic "responsabilité" (responsibility) without governance connotation
- "imputabilité" (imputability) - rarely used, less forceful
- Concept often omitted entirely

**Interpretation:**  
The complete absence of "accountability" in French suggests a shift from **individual accountability** (Anglo-American governance tradition) to **collective responsibility** (Continental European tradition). This is not merely linguistic preference but reflects different governance philosophies.

---

### 2. "Control" vs "Monitoring" - Regulatory Framing

| Concept | English Term | Count | French Term | Count | Ratio |
|---------|--------------|-------|-------------|-------|-------|
| Oversight | monitoring | 5 | contrôle | 27 | **5.4:1 FR** |
| | oversight | 7 | surveillance | 16 | 2.3:1 FR |

**Interpretation:**  
French versions emphasize **regulatory control** ("contrôle") over **observational monitoring**. This suggests:
- English: Watch and observe (passive oversight)
- French: Active intervention and control (regulatory power)

**Example Divergence:**
- EN: "continuous monitoring of AI systems"
- FR: "contrôle continu des systèmes d'IA" (continuous control)

---

### 3. "Bias" - Technical vs Moral Framing

| Language | Term | Count | Framing |
|----------|------|-------|---------|
| English | bias | 34 | Technical/algorithmic |
| French | biais | 22 | Mixed technical/social |
| French | préjugé | 9 | Moral/social prejudice |

**Key Difference:**  
- English uses "bias" primarily in technical contexts (algorithmic bias, data bias)
- French introduces "préjugé" (prejudice), adding moral/ethical dimension absent in English

**Example:**
- EN: "mitigating algorithmic bias in the model"
- FR: "atténuer les préjugés et biais algorithmiques" (mitigating prejudices and algorithmic biases)

---

### 4. "Fairness" - Philosophical Divergence

| Language | Term | Count | Philosophical Root |
|----------|------|-------|-------------------|
| English | fairness | 31 | Anglo-American equity |
| French | équité | 30 | Continental justice |

**Analysis:**  
Nearly equivalent counts (31 vs 30) but **different semantic fields**:
- "Fairness" → procedural justice, equal treatment
- "Équité" → substantive justice, proportional outcomes

## 🌍 Cross-Cultural Governance Implications

### Governance Philosophy Differences

**Anglo-American (English) Emphasis:**
- Individual accountability
- Passive monitoring/observation
- Technical bias mitigation
- Procedural fairness
- Clear lines of responsibility

**Continental European (French) Emphasis:**
- Collective responsibility
- Active regulatory control
- Moral prejudice awareness
- Substantive equity
- Systemic oversight mechanisms

### Translation is Transformation

These findings demonstrate that translation of governance documents is **not neutral transfer** but **conceptual transformation**. Key governance concepts are:
- **Omitted** (accountability)
- **Amplified** (control/contrôle)
- **Reframed** (bias → bias + prejudice)
- **Shifted** (monitoring → control)

## 🔬 Methodology

### Term Selection Process

1. **Literature Review:** Identified key governance concepts from AI ethics frameworks (EU HLEG, OECD, IEEE)
2. **Bilingual Lexicons:** Consulted official Canadian government translation databases
3. **Expert Validation:** Terms reviewed against academic governance literature
4. **Iterative Refinement:** Added terms based on initial corpus analysis

### Analysis Pipeline

```
PDF Documents (n=32)
    ↓
Text Extraction (pdfminer.six)
    ↓
Term Frequency Counting
    ↓
Context Extraction (±50 chars)
    ↓
LLM Semantic Analysis (GPT-4)
    ↓
Statistical Comparison
    ↓
Visualization Generation
```

### Quality Assurance

- Manual review of 10% of term occurrences (spot-checking)
- Cross-validation with CSV data exports
- LLM confidence scores reviewed
- False positive rate estimated at <5%
