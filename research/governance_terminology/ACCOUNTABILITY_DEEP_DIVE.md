# Accountability Gap: EN vs FR Deep Dive

## Executive Summary

The term "accountability" appears **15 times** in English AI Impact Assessments but **0 times** in French versions.

Instead, French uses three distinct terms:
- **responsabilité** (1 occurrence): Legal/moral responsibility
- **reddition de comptes** (11 occurrences): Accountability as financial/administrative reporting
- **imputabilité** (4 occurrences): Attributability of outcomes (technical responsibility)

---

## Key Finding: Three Different Accountability Concepts

| French Term | Frequency | Concept | Context |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **responsabilité** | 1 | Legal responsibility | Regulatory/international governance frameworks |
| **reddition de comptes** | 11 | Reporting accountability | Administrative processes, decision documentation |
| **imputabilité** | 4 | Attributability | Technical/algorithmic responsibility, traceability |

**vs. English:**
- **accountability** | 15 | Unified governance concept | Spans legal, administrative, AND technical domains |

## Interpretation

### Why Does This Matter?

1. **Semantic Coverage:** English "accountability" is a **superordinate term** covering all aspects of responsibility in algorithmic governance.

2. **French Specificity:** French documents use **subordinate terms** that carve up accountability into distinct legal, administrative, and technical domains.

3. **Policy Implication:** 
   - EN: "Ensure accountability" = manage all responsibility aspects holistically
   - FR: "Assurer la reddition de comptes" = document administrative processes
   - FR: "Établir l'imputabilité" = trace technical causation

4. **Governance Philosophy:**
   - EN: Unified accountability framework
   - FR: Domain-specific responsibility types reflecting Québec civil law tradition + federal administrative law
