# Semantic Drift in Bilingual AI Governance Documents

**Analysis Date:** January 9, 2026
**Documents Analyzed:** 16 bilingual pairs

## Executive Summary

This report documents semantic drift between English and French versions of Canadian AI Impact Assessments.

## Key Findings by Project

### 1. ATIP Digital Services

**Department:** Treasury Board Secretariat

**Semantic Differences:**

- **EN:** `Monitoring` → **FR:** `Surveillance`
  - While 'monitoring' in English conveys a sense of routine or regular observation, 'surveillance' in French may imply a more intensive or vigilant observation, potentially suggesting a higher level of scrutiny.

- **EN:** `Human-in-the-loop for decisions` → **FR:** `Maillon humain de la prise de décisions`
  - 'Human-in-the-loop' emphasizes the role of human judgment within automated processes, whereas 'Maillon humain' (human link) in French highlights the human element as a part of a chain, possibly suggesting a more integrated role in the process.

- **EN:** `Testing` → **FR:** `Mise à l’essai`
  - 'Testing' in English is a broad term that can encompass various forms of evaluation, while 'Mise à l’essai' specifically implies a trial or experimental phase, potentially indicating a more structured or formalized testing process.

**Assessment:** The translation between English and French maintains a high level of semantic alignment with key governance terms accurately translated. However, there are subtle semantic differences in terms such as 'monitoring' versus 'surveillance' and 'human-in-the-loop' versus 'maillon humain', which could reflect slight variations in perceived accountability and oversight. These differences are minor and do not significantly impact the overall understanding of the document's intent. The translation quality is high, ensuring that the essence and specificity of governance terminology are preserved across both languages.
